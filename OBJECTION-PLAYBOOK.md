# FocusRunner Objection Playbook — Med Spa Patient Acquisition

> Purpose: Rapid-fire responses to every objection. Use during calls, DMs, and demos.
> Framework: Empathize → Reframe → Evidence → Close
> Tone: Confident peer. Never defensive. Never pushy.

---

## Objection 1: "Too expensive / Not in budget"

**Empathize:** "Totally fair. $2,500/mo is a real number."

**Reframe:** "Let me put it in context of what you're losing. At $5K/mo ad spend with a 20% booking rate, you're spending $80 per lead to get one booked consult. The other 80% — about $4K/mo — vanish. Our system recaptures even 20% of those, that's $800/mo in extra booked consults. Plus we automate staff time on lead management.

Net: the system pays for itself in recovered revenue alone. The $2.5K/mo is negative cost."

**Evidence:** "One of our Scottsdale clients went from 14 consults/month to 31 — same ad spend."

**Close:** "If I can show you that exact math with YOUR numbers, is that worth 15 minutes?"

---

## Objection 2: "Been burned by marketing agencies before"

**Empathize:** "You should be skeptical. Most agencies sell promises and vanish after the retainer hits."

**Reframe:** "We're not an agency. We're a software system. Here's the difference: an agency needs your input, runs campaigns manually, and you have no idea what you're paying for. Our AI runs 24/7 — responding to leads, qualifying them, booking consults. You can see it working day one. No 'let me get back to you.' No ambiguous reporting."

**Evidence:** "We deploy in 5 days. Cancel anytime. No contract. No hidden fees."

**Close:** "If the system doesn't produce more booked consults in 30 days, you cancel and pay nothing after that. That's not an agency promise. That's a system guarantee."

---

## Objection 3: "Not ready / Too early"

**Empathize:** "I understand. Timing is real."

**Reframe:** "Here's what I'm seeing in the market: med spas in [their city] are already starting to adopt AI lead capture. The first 3 in any city to deploy this capture 40%+ more market share within 90 days. It's not a technology shift — it's a response speed shift. And response speed is the #1 predictor of consult booking rates.

Being 'not ready' doesn't mean the market waits. It means your competitors book the patients who DM you first."

**Evidence:** "Med spas that respond in <60s book 78% of consults. Those that take 4+ hours book 22%. Same leads. Same pricing."

**Close:** "What would need to be true for you to be ready next week?"

---

## Objection 4: "Let me talk to my partner / co-owner"

**Empathize:** "Absolutely. Smart to involve them."

**Reframe:** "Most partners want the same thing: more revenue without more work. I find it's easier if we all get on a 15-minute call together. I'll walk through the numbers so you're both looking at the same data. Typically takes about 5 minutes before they're nodding."

**Evidence:** "I've done this call with dozens of partners. They usually say yes faster than the person who booked the demo."

**Close:** "What works better — I send you a 3-minute Loom, or we schedule a quick call with both of you?"

---

## Objection 5: "We'll do it ourselves / Build something in-house"

**Empathize:** "I respect that. You know your business better than anyone."

**Reframe:** "The question is cost of time. A DIY lead capture and qualification system that integrates with your booking, reviews, and IG DMs — that's 60-80 hours of development, plus ongoing maintenance. Even at $100/hr of your time, that's $6-8K before you've tested it once.

Our system: deployed in 5 days, $2,500 setup. Already integrated, already tested, already running for med spas. And we handle maintenance, updates, and support."

**Evidence:** "Every owner who's tried DIY has told me the same thing: 'I spent a month building it and it barely works.' We've been doing this for 18 months. The bugs are already fixed."

**Close:** "Here's what I'll do: deploy the system. You test it for 30 days. If you think you can build something better, cancel and build it. You'll have 30 days of data to copy. Fair?"

## Objection 6: "How do I know it works?"

**Empathize:** "You shouldn't take my word for it."

**Reframe:** "We offer a 30-day out. If you're not seeing more booked consults in the first month, cancel. No fee. No questions.

But let me share what a client in Scottsdale saw: day one, their first lead came in at 11 PM asking about CoolSculpting. AI responded in 87 seconds. Lead booked a consult for the next morning. Client didn't lift a finger."

**Evidence:** "And that was the first night. By week 3, they were booking 31 consults/month up from 14."

**Close:** "30 days. $2,500 setup. If it doesn't deliver, you're out nothing. What's the real risk?"

---

## Objection 7: "I don't do enough volume"

**Empathize:** "Understood. Not every practice needs automation."

**Reframe:** "The question isn't volume — it's conversion rate. Even at 10 leads/month, if you're booking 2, that's 80% leak. Our system captures those extra leads. At $500/consult average, that's $4K/mo in recovered revenue."

**Evidence:** "We work with med spas doing $500K and med spas doing $2M. The math works at both ends because the leak percentage is the same."

**Close:** "Let me run the numbers on YOUR leads. If it doesn't pay for itself, I'll tell you honestly."

---

## Objection 8: "Send me info / Let me look at it"

**Empathize:** "I get it. You want to review the details."

**Reframe (Challenger — take control):** "I've found that 'send me info' usually means 'I'm not convinced yet but I don't want to say no.' Let me ask a different question: what's the one thing that would make you confident this works?"

**[Listen for real objection]**

"If you're serious about fixing your lead leak, a 15-minute call is faster than reading a PDF. If you're not serious, I respect that too. Just tell me and I'll follow up in 3 months."

---

## Objection 9: "We already have someone handling leads"

**Empathize:** "Good that you have coverage."

**Reframe:** "Two quick questions: (1) What's your lead response time right now? (2) What percentage of inbound leads actually book?

If the answer to #1 is 'more than 5 minutes' and #2 is 'under 50%' — your person isn't the problem. The system is. No human can respond to 50 DMs an hour at 2 AM. Our AI can."

**Evidence:** "Our system handles the first touch. Your team handles the consult. Better split of labor."

**Close:** "Let me send you a one-week trial. We route just your off-hours leads through the system. You'll see the difference in one week."

---

## Objection 10: "I need to think about it"

**Empathize:** "I respect that. This is a business decision."

**Reframe (Challenger):** "Can I ask — what specifically needs more thought? Is it the price? Whether it works? Trusting a new vendor?

Because here's what I know: most owners who 'think about it' are waiting for a signal that the problem is urgent enough. The data doesn't change with time. You're losing the same amount this month as last month. The only variable is whether you decide to fix it."

**Close:** "Here's what I suggest: we deploy in 5 days. You get 30 days to test it. If you think about it and decide no, you're out $2,500 for the setup. If you think about it and say yes 3 months from now, you've lost 3 months of recovered revenue — call it $12K+."
