## Part 2: The Three Gaps

### Gap 1: Response Time

| | Your Practice | Industry Average | Best Practice |
|--|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Response time to new lead | ________ hours | 4-24 hours | <60 seconds |
| % of leads answered within 5 min | ________% | ~20% | 95%+ |
| **Estimated leads lost to slow response** | **~________/month** | **70% of all leads** | — |

**The math:** For every 100 leads, 78 book the first spa that responds. If you're not the first, you lose that patient — potentially forever.

### Gap 2: Follow-Up

| | Your Practice | Best Practice |
|--|--------------|---------------|
| Number of follow-up touches per lead | ________ | 5+ touches over 30 days |
| Follow-up interval (hours between touches) | ________ | 24-72 hours |
| **Estimated leads lost to no follow-up** | **~________/month** | 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups |

**The math:** Most med spas send 1 text or make 1 call. If the lead doesn't answer, they're marked "dead." But 80% of sales happen between touch 3 and touch 5. You're quitting before the sale happens.

| | Your Practice | Best Practice |
|--|--------------|---------------|
| % of front desk time spent on phone/lead intake | ________% | <20% (AI handles intake) |
| % of inquiries that are tire-kickers vs. real patients | ________% | AI filters 60%+ of non-buyers |

**The math:** Front desk staff spend 60%+ of their time answering "How much does Botox cost?" from people who never book. High-value patients (filler, laser, RF microneedling at $800-$2K/visit) get the same treatment as tire-kickers.
