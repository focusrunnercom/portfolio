# FocusRunner Call Script — $2,500 AI Patient Acquisition System

> Target: Med Spa Owners ($500K–$3M annual revenue)
> Offer: AI Patient Acquisition System — $2,500 setup + $2,500/mo
> Framework: SPIN (Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff) + Challenger (Teach, Tailor, Take Control)
> Calling Style: Peer-to-peer business owner. Confident. Direct. Outcome-driven.
> Language: ENGLISH ONLY. Board mandate. No exceptions.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Call Research (5 minutes — mandatory)

Before dialing, know these 3 things:
1. Their recent Instagram posts + engagement rate (last 7 days)
2. Google Business Profile review count + response rate
3. Any ads they're running (Meta Ad Library)

If you can't answer these 3, you're calling blind. Don't.

## Phase 2: Discovery — SPIN (0:45 – 4:00)

### SITUATION Questions (Establish baseline)

Goal: Get them talking about their current state. Do not pitch yet.

**Q1:** "How many consults are you booking per month from your current marketing?"

**Q2:** "What's your ad spend per month?"

**Q3:** "How long does it take your team to respond to a lead that comes in through IG or your website?"

**Q4:** "Roughly what percentage of leads actually book?"

**Q5:** "How many people on your team handle lead follow-up?"

---

### PROBLEM Questions (Surface the pain)

Goal: Let them discover the gap themselves by probing specific friction points.

**P1:** "When a lead comes in at 9 PM asking about Botox pricing — what happens? Who responds, and how fast?"

**P2:** "What happens to leads that don't book on the first contact?"

**P3:** "How much time per week does your front desk spend answering 'how much is X?' questions from people who never book?"

**P4:** "Have you ever calculated what a lead is worth to you per treatment type?"

**P5:** "What's the last thing you tried to fix this, and why didn't it work?"

---

### IMPLICATION Questions (Quantify the cost)

Goal: Make the cost of inaction real. Use THEIR numbers.

**I1:** "So if you're spending $[ad spend] and booking 20% of leads — the other 80% at $[avg consult value] each is $[amount] in potential revenue every month. What's happening to those leads right now?"

**I2:** "At [$avg consult value] per consult and [$current booking rate], adding even 10 more consults per month would mean [$amount] in additional revenue. What would that do for your practice?"

**I3:** "Your front desk spends [$hours] hours/week on non-booking inquiries. At $[pay rate]/hour for that role, that's $[monthly cost] in payroll — for leads that don't book. What else could that time go toward?"

**I4:** "Your competitor in [neighborhood] just automated this. They're now responding to every lead in under 60 seconds. If 78% of consults go to the fastest responder — what happens when the rest of your market catches on?"

---

### NEED-PAYOFF Questions (Let them sell themselves)

Goal: Have them state the solution before you present it.

**N1:** "If you had a system that caught every lead within 90 seconds, qualified them by treatment value, and booked consults automatically — so your team only talks to patients who are ready to book — what would that do for your numbers?"

**N2:** "If that same system followed up with cold leads for 30 days until they book or opt out — how many more consults do you think you'd close?"

**N3:** "What if you could get 40% more consults without hiring a single person or spending a dollar more on ads?"

**[Let them answer. They'll tell you the ROI you need to deliver.]**

## Phase 3: The Teach — Challenger Insight (4:00 – 6:00)

Goal: Present a framework they haven't seen. Own the expertise.

---

**Transition:** "Let me show you something most med spa owners don't realize."

**The 78/22 Rule:**

"There's a data point from the med spa space that changes how I think about marketing entirely. I call it the 78/22 rule.

The first med spa to respond to a lead books 78% of the time. The second responder — even if they're faster by reputation, better location, lower price — books 22%. This holds across 2,000+ med spa transactions we've analyzed.

Your practice might have the best injectors in [city]. Better before/afters. Better reviews. But if you take 4 hours to respond and a competitor takes 60 seconds — they win. Not because they're better. Because they're faster.

Most owners think marketing is about getting more traffic. It's not. Traffic is abundant. The competitive advantage is what happens AFTER the lead arrives.

**We've built a three-layer AI system that makes you the fastest responder in your market:**

1. **Capture Layer** — Catches every lead from IG, website, Google, ads within 90 seconds
2. **Qualification Layer** — Asks revenue-focused questions (budget, timeline, treatment type) and pre-screens before any human touches it
3. **Booking Layer** — Automatically schedules consults for qualified patients, follows up on cold leads for 30 days

**The result:** Our clients average 30-50% more consults in 60 days with zero ad spend increase.

**One example:** A med spa in Scottsdale went from 14 consults/month to 31. Same ad spend. They added $34K in revenue in month one alone."

## Phase 5: Objection Handling (As needed)

---

### TOO EXPENSIVE

"$2,500/mo is $83/day. One booked consult covers it. Our clients average 12-17 additional consults per month. That's $12K-$17K in additional revenue against a $2,500 cost. You're not spending money — you're buying booked patients at 15-20% of what you're paying per consult today.

What's the real concern — the investment or whether it'll work?"

### ALREADY HAVE ENOUGH PATIENTS

"Good — that means your system works. Here's the thing: having enough patients today doesn't protect you from competitors who are automating their response times right now. In 90 days, 6 med spas in your market will have 60-second response systems. You'll still have 'enough patients' — but your share will shrink.

First movers capture 40% more market share. You can keep 'enough' or take more. Which do you want?"

### TRIED AI CHATBOTS / IT DIDN'T WORK

"Ninety-nine percent of 'AI for med spas' products are generic chatbots that say 'how can I help you?' — which patients ignore because it sounds like a robot.

Ours works differently. It qualifies against revenue criteria. It asks treatment-specific questions. It books consults by treatment type and value. It's a sales system running on AI, not a chatbot on your website.

Most owners who say 'AI didn't work' tried the wrong product. Show me what you tried and I'll show you where it failed compared to what we build."

### NOT RIGHT NOW / TIMING IS BAD

"What changed between now and a month from now? Your leads are still going cold. Your staff is still spending hours on unqualified questions. Your competitors are moving.

First movers in any market capture 40% more search volume and 78% of fast-response bookings. Every month you wait, someone else takes that share.

This deploys in 5 days. If it doesn't work in 30, you pay nothing for the setup. You're protected. The only risk is waiting until your competitors are already set up."

### NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT

"What specifically do you need to think through? I can answer it right now.

Or — would it help if I sent you the performance dashboard from a current deployment? You can see real numbers from a med spa similar to yours, and we can talk again in 48 hours."

### NEED TO TALK TO MY PARTNER

"Totally fair. Most smart decisions involve a partner. Let me make that conversation easier.

Here's what I'd recommend you share: the cost is $83/day. The average result is 12+ additional consults/month. Even at half that — 6 consults — at $1,000/consult average that's $6K in revenue against $2.5K cost.

We can jump on a 3-way call with your partner right now. Or I'll send over a one-page summary and follow up in 2 days. Which works better?"

## Closing Script Variations

| Situation | Best Close |
|-----------|-----------|
| Warm, engaged, no objections | Hard Close (Phase 7) |
| Hesitant about investment | Soft Close — "Let me build it for you to see" |
| Needs partner validation | Send materials + offer 3-way call |
| Sold but wants to compare | Send dashboard + comparison + follow-up in 48h |
| Showed interest but vague | "What specifically's holding you back?" then pick objection |
| Price objection at close | Return to Phase 5 — "Is it the investment or confidence?" |
