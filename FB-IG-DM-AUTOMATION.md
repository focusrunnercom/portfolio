# Facebook / Instagram DM Automation Script — AI Patient Acquisition

**Owner:** Copywriter (Schwartz Framework: 4 Forces of Mass Desire)
**Channel:** Facebook Messenger + Instagram Direct
**Platform:** ManyChat / Chatfuel / GHL Conversations (API-connected)
**Model:** GPT-4o-mini (fast, cheap, human-tone)
**Language:** English only. Board mandate.
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-13

---

## Why DM Matters

40% of med spa leads come through Facebook Messenger or Instagram DMs. Most med spas respond from a phone — 4+ hours later. By then the lead is at a competitor.

This script runs 24/7 inside the DM channel. No link-in-bio redirect. No "call our number." The conversation happens where the lead already is.

---

## Schwartz 4 Forces Applied

| Force | Application in DM |
|-------|------------------|
| **Force 1: Desire** — The lead wants results, not software. They want younger-looking skin, less fat, more confidence. Every message mirrors that outcome. |
| **Force 2: Identification** — The lead must see themselves. "You're probably here because you've been thinking about X." If they don't identify, they ghost. |
| **Force 3: Credibility** — Real numbers, not claims. "Our clients average a 38.7% booking rate" beats "We help you get more patients." |
| **Force 4: Action** — The DMs either book a consult or get qualified for follow-up. No dead-end conversations. |

---

## Flow Overview

```
Incoming DM → Auto-Greeting → Interest Discovery → Qualification → Booking / Nurture
                                                                     ↓
                                                              Human handoff (Sales)
```

## 1. Auto-Greeting (First Message)

**Trigger:** Lead sends first DM to the page (any message)

**AI Response:**

> Hey! Welcome to [Spa Name]. I'm the AI concierge — I can answer questions, check availability, and get you booked in under 60 seconds.
>
> Quick question so I point you in the right direction — what treatment are you curious about?
>
> 1. Botox / Fillers
> 2. Laser / Skin tightening
> 3. Body contouring / Sculpting
> 4. IV Therapy / Wellness
> 5. Something else

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 1 (Desire):** "booked in under 60 seconds" — speed is the benefit they want
- **Force 2 (Identification):** Force them to self-identify which bucket they belong to
- **No gatekeeping:** No "call our number" or "visit our website." The conversation is inside the DM.

---

## 2. First-Time vs Returning Discovery

**If "Botox / Fillers" selected:**

> Great choice. Quick follow-up — is this your first time getting injectables, or have you done it before?

**If first time:**

> Perfect. A few things our first-timers usually want to know:
>
> 1. How much does it cost? (It depends on units — but most first-timers spend $400-$800 for a great result)
> 2. Does it hurt? (Quick pinch. Most people describe it as a 2/10. Ice numbs the area first.)
> 3. How long does it last? (Botox: 3-4 months. Fillers: 6-18 months depending on type.)
>
> Which one do you want me to dive into?

**If returning:**

> Awesome — we love seeing familiar faces! When was your last treatment?
>
> 1. Within the last 3 months
> 2. 3-6 months ago
> 3. 6+ months ago (time for a refresh!)
>
> And are you looking to book with the same provider you saw last time?

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 2 (Identification):** First-timer vs returning splits the audience. Each gets the exact message they need.
- **Force 3 (Credibility):** Real price ranges, real timeframes. Not "affordable prices" or "long-lasting results."
- **Force 1 (Desire):** First-timers get their three biggest objections answered before they ask.

## 3. Qualification Flow (Lead Gen Path)

**Trigger:** Lead expresses interest in booking OR asks a pricing question

**AI Response:**

> I can help with that! Let me grab a couple quick details so I can have the right person reach out:
>
> 1. What's your name?
> 2. What's the best number to text you?
> 3. When were you thinking of coming in?
>    - This week
>    - Next week
>    - Just browsing, no rush

**After all 3 answered:**

> Amazing — thanks [Name]. Based on what you told me, here's what I recommend next:
>
> [CTA depending on response]
>
> **If "This week / Next week":** "I have [day] at [time] and [day] at [time] available. Which works better? I'll lock it in right now."
>
> **If "Just browsing":** "Totally understand. Here's what most first-timers do — they come in for a free consultation. No commitment, just a chat with our provider about what would work best for them. Want me to grab one of the open slots this month?"

**Phone number capture rationale:** DM notifications are unreliable. A lead that doesn't book in-session needs an SMS follow-up. Get the phone early, keep the relationship outside Meta's algorithm.

## 4. Botox/Fillers Pricing Script (Most Common DM)

**Trigger:** "How much does Botox cost?" or any pricing question

**AI Response:**

> Great question. Here's the honest answer:
>
> Botox is priced per unit. Most first-timers spend $400-$800 depending on:
> - Forehead lines: 10-20 units
> - Crow's feet: 10-15 units per side
> - Frown lines (11s): 15-25 units
>
> At [Spa Name], we're at $XX/unit with a complimentary consultation included. A lot of our patients say they wish they'd done it sooner.
>
> Want me to check what appointments we have this week? Or would you rather start with the free consultation?

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 3 (Credibility):** Unit prices, not "book a consultation to find out." Shows you're transparent.
- **Force 1 (Desire):** "Wish they'd done it sooner." Subtle social proof from existing patients.
- **Force 4 (Action):** Two clear paths: book now or consultation.

---

## 5. Competitor Comparison Objection

**Trigger:** "Why should I come here instead of [Competitor]?"

**AI Response:**

> Fair question. Here's what I'd tell you if we were talking in person:
>
> A lot of med spas offer the same treatments at similar prices. The difference is the experience.
>
> At [Spa Name], you get:
> - [Provider credential, e.g., "10+ years of injectable experience"]
> - [Specific differentiator, e.g., "Complimentary numbing + aftercare kit"]
> - [Social proof, e.g., "4.9 stars on Google from 200+ reviews"]
>
> But honestly, the best way to know if we're right for you is to come in and see for yourself. Free consultation, no pressure. If you love it, great. If not, no hard feelings.
>
> Want me to grab a slot this week?

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 2 (Identification):** Meets the objection head-on instead of deflecting.
- **Force 3 (Credibility):** Real credentials, real reviews. Not "we're the best."
- **Force 4 (Action):** Low-friction next step.

## 6. Ghost Recovery (Lead Went Silent)

**Trigger:** Lead was active, stopped replying mid-conversation. Wait 4 hours.

**AI Message (auto-send after 4h silence):**

> Hey! No pressure — just didn't want to leave you hanging.
>
> If you'd rather browse on your own first, here's our treatment menu: [Link to menu page]
>
> Or if you want to come in and chat face-to-face, I have open slots this week: [Booking link]
>
> Either way, we're here when you're ready.

**If no response for 24h (SMS follow-up via captured number):**

> [Name], it's [Spa Name] here — we were chatting on Instagram about [Treatment] and lost the thread. If you're still interested, I can grab you a free consultation slot. Just reply YES and I'll send the times.
>
> Reply STOP to opt out.

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 4 (Action):** Two escape routes — browse alone or book.
- **Force 3 (Credibility):** No "are you still there?" or desperate follow-up. Calm, confident.
- **DM to SMS bridge:** Keep the relationship going when the DM thread dies.

## 7. Review Request (Post-Visit)

**Trigger:** 24 hours after booked appointment

**AI Message (SMS, not DM):**

> [Name], how was your visit to [Spa Name]?
>
> If you loved it, would you mind leaving a quick Google review? Takes 30 seconds and it helps other patients find us: [Google Review Link]
>
> If anything wasn't perfect, reply back and I'll make sure the right person sees it.
>
> — [Spa Name] AI Concierge

---

## 8. Rebooking Sequence (Post-Treatment)

**Trigger:** 2.5 months after Botox treatment, 5 months after filler

**AI Message (SMS):**

> [Name] — quick check-in from [Spa Name]. It's been about [X months] since your last treatment, which means your results might be starting to fade.
>
> Most of our patients come back right around now for a refresh. I have [Provider Name]'s first available next week.
>
> Want me to book your same treatment? Reply "yes" and I'll send you the times.

**Why this works (Schwartz):**
- **Force 1 (Desire):** The results are fading. The desire to look good again kicks in.
- **Force 2 (Identification):** "Most of our patients come back right around now" — I should too.
- **Force 4 (Action):** One-word reply to book. Lowest possible friction.

## 10. System Prompt (ManyChat / Chatfuel / API)

```
You are a medical spa intake concierge for {SPA_NAME}. Your job is to book appointments.

RULES:
1. Never say "our AI system" or sound robotic. You are the spa's digital front desk.
2. Never redirect to a phone number. Keep the conversation in DMs.
3. If the lead wants to book: get name + phone + preferred time. Lock it in.
4. If the lead asks pricing: give real numbers, unit prices, ranges. Be transparent.
5. If the lead compares competitors: acknowledge, state your differentiators, offer free consultation.
6. If the lead goes silent 4h: send ghost recovery. If 24h: call their phone (if captured).
7. Always end with a clear next step. Never leave the conversation open-ended.
8. Tone: Warm consultant. Informed friend. Not pushy, not robotic.
9. Language: English only. No exceptions.

QUALIFICATION OUTPUT (capture to CRM):
{
  "name": "",
  "phone": "",
  "treatment_interest": "botox|fillers|laser|body|other",
  "first_time": true|false,
  "timeline": "this_week|next_week|browsing",
  "source": "facebook_dm|instagram_dm",
  "conversation_summary": ""
}
```
