# FocusRunner Sales Scripts — Cold Outreach Playbook

> Niche: Local Service Businesses (plumbers, roofers, HVAC, electricians, dentists, med spas, lawyers, pest control, landscapers)
> Framework: Hook → Qualification → Pain → Offer → Close
> Tone: Direct, peer-to-peer, outcome-driven. No corporate-speak.

## 4. Discovery Call Script

**Goal:** Identify pain, quantify the gap, sell the vision — NOT the features.

**Call Flow (25 minutes):**

### Opening (2 min)

"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this tight. My goal today is to figure out if what we do is actually a fit for your business — and if it's not, I'll tell you straight up. Sound fair?"

### The 80/20 Rule — Let Them Talk (15 min)

"Walk me through your current setup. How are new customers finding you right now?"

Listen for:
- Google/Organic (do they track it?)
- Referrals (systematic or haphazard?)
- Paid ads (who manages it?)
- Word of mouth (predictable?)

"Of those — which channel do you wish was working better?"

"What happens when a lead comes in? Paint me the picture. Someone calls — who picks up? What if they email? What if it's after hours?"

Key insight to uncover: RESPONSE TIME. Almost no local service business responds to leads in under 30 minutes. This is THE advantage.

"How many leads are you getting in a typical week? And how many of those turn into booked jobs?"

This gives you: lead volume and conversion rate. If they don't know — that's the pain point.

"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how you get new customers, what would it be?"

Let them articulate their own problem. This is your ammunition for the close.

### The Shift (3 min)

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I'm hearing: [summarize their pain in their words]. Does that sound right?"

Wait for agreement.

"Most [niche] owners I talk to have the same challenge. The good news is this is exactly what we solve."

### The Pitch (3 min)

"We take everything that's falling through the cracks — the slow responses, the missed follow-ups, the unanswered reviews — and we put AI on it. Here's what that looks like in practice:"

[Share 1-2 relevant outcomes from your case studies]

"The typical result: 20-40% more qualified calls in the first 30 days, without increasing ad spend."

### The Close (2 min)

"Here's what I'd propose: we run a 30-day pilot. I'll set up the full system — lead response, review management, follow-up automation. You'll see exactly how many additional booked jobs it generates. If at the end of 30 days you don't think it's worth it, we part ways. Fair?"

"If you're in, I can have this live for you within 72 hours. Want to shake on it?"

## 5. Objection Handling — Top 5

### Objection 1: "I don't have the budget for this right now."

**Reframe:** This isn't a cost — it's a revenue unlock.

"I hear you. But let me flip the math on this. If you're getting, say, 30 leads a week and converting 30% — that's 9 booked jobs. If we move that to 40% — 12 booked jobs. What's an average job worth to you? [Wait for answer] So those 3 extra jobs per week — that's [$X] in new revenue. Per week. And you're telling me you can't afford [price]?"

**If they're genuinely tight:**

"Totally understand. What if we structure this as a revenue share? You only pay when the system generates a booked job. Low risk, high upside. Want me to run those numbers?"

### Objection 2: "I've tried marketing agencies before — they never deliver."

**Reframe:** We're not an agency. We're AI infrastructure.

"I get that. And honestly, most agencies are selling you billable hours, not outcomes. We're different because we don't do creative — we do systems. The AI does the work, and you see the results in your call volume. No monthly retainers for 'strategy sessions.' Just: more leads → more booked jobs → more revenue. I'll show you the dashboard. You'll see every lead, every response, every conversion. Nothing hidden."

### Objection 3: "I'm too small for AI. That's for big companies."

**Reframe:** You're the ideal size. Big companies are slow.

"That's actually exactly why this works for you. Big companies have 12 layers of approval just to change a process. You can decide today and have it running by Friday. Plus, you're not replacing anyone — you just don't have a marketing person to begin with. We're giving you marketing capabilities without the salary."

### Objection 4: "Let me think about it."

**Reframe:** What's the real objection?

"'Think about it' usually means there's something you're not sure about. Is it the price? The commitment? Or do you just need to see proof that it works for someone exactly like you?"

Wait for answer. If it's proof:

"Fair. Here's what I'll do — talk to [Client Name], our roofing client in [City]. He was skeptical too. I'll have him call you directly. No sales pitch from me, just an owner-to-owner conversation. If after that you're still not sure, no hard feelings."

### Objection 5: "I'm happy with how things are. We're busy enough."

**Reframe:** Busy doesn't mean optimized.

"Totally respect that. One question though — of the leads you're getting now, how many do you think you're losing because of slow response time? [Wait] The data says 78% of local service leads go to whoever responds first. Not whoever's best — whoever's fastest. If you're happy with a 22% shot at every lead that comes in, no need for what we do. But if you want to flip those odds..."

"Also — 'busy enough' today is one thing. But what happens when your top competitor deploys AI and starts scooping leads you never even saw? I'd rather you have the system in place before that happens."

## 6. Qualification Framework

**Use this before investing time in a discovery call:**

### BANT-Qualified (High-Value)

| Criterion | Threshold | Red Flag |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **B**udget | Discussing marketing investment | "I don't spend anything on marketing" |
| **A**uthority | Owner / Founder / Decision-maker | "I need to check with my partner" |
| **N**eed | Missing leads, slow response, no reviews management | "We already have someone handling this" |
| **T**iming | Now or next 30 days | "Maybe next year" |

### Lead Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 🔥 Hot | BANT all green + expressed urgency | Book discovery call within 48 hours |
| ☀️ Warm | BANT 3/4 green + interested | Nurture: share case study + follow up in 5 days |
| 🌤️ Lukewarm | BANT 2/4 green or "not now" | Drip: add to monthly newsletter + check in quarterly |
| ❄️ Cold | "Not interested" or no response | Move on. Archive. Revisit in 6 months. |

## 7. Voice & Tone Guidelines

**When reaching out to local service owners:**

- **Don't sound like a marketer.** They smell that a mile away. Sound like another business owner who figured something out.
- **Respect their craft.** They're plumbers, roofers, dentists. They're proud of their work. Acknowledge it.
- **Talk outcomes, not features.** "More booked jobs" beats "AI-powered lead nurture sequences" every time.
- **Use their language.** If they say "jobs" not "projects" — you say "jobs." Mirror their vocabulary.
- **Be direct.** These are busy people. If you can say it in 3 sentences, don't use 8.
- **Social proof is your only weapon.** "One of our roofing clients in Austin added $47K last month" beats any sales technique.
