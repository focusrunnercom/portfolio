Cost & Valuation

How much does it cost to buy a med spa?

Med spas typically sell for 2.5–4.5× SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings). A practice doing $1.5M revenue with 30% margins ($450K SDE) trades between $1.1M and $2.0M. The multiple depends on recurring revenue percentage, provider diversity, and whether a documented patient acquisition system exists. Practices with AI acquisition systems command 0.5–1.0× higher multiples.

What's the minimum down payment?

SBA 7(a) loans require 10–20% down. On a $1.5M acquisition, expect $150K–$300K down. Seller financing (20–40% seller note) can reduce the cash requirement. Earnouts tied to post-close revenue targets further reduce upfront capital.

How are med spas valued?

Three methods: (1) SDE multiple — most common under $3M revenue, 2.5–4.5×; (2) EBITDA multiple — used above $3M, 4–7×; (3) Revenue multiple — quick benchmark, 0.7–1.5× annual revenue. Read our full valuation guide.

Finding Deals

How do I find med spas for sale?

The best deals come through relationships, not public listings: specialized healthcare M&A brokers, vendor relationships (Allergan, Galderma, laser manufacturers), direct outreach to practice owners, and aesthetic conferences. Most profitable acquisitions are off-market. Read the complete acquisition guide.

What's the best market to buy in?

Sun Belt states dominate: Florida (Miami, Tampa), Texas (Dallas, Austin, Houston), Arizona (Phoenix, Scottsdale), Nevada (Las Vegas). These markets have high med spa density, strong population growth, and favorable regulatory environments. Nashville and the Southeast are emerging as undervalued markets.

What is AI patient acquisition?

A 4-layer system: (1) AI chatbot qualifies leads 24/7 on your website, (2) automated SMS/email sequences nurture and book consultations, (3) AI optimizes ad campaigns by channel and audience, (4) predictive models identify at-risk patients before they churn. Practices using AI acquire patients at $40–$120/lead vs. $185–$320 industry average. Read the AI marketing playbook.

7 days from kickoff to live. Day 1: audit and strategy. Days 2–5: build landing page, configure AI chatbot, integrate SMS/email, set up CRM pipeline. Days 6–7: test, launch, verify tracking. Month 1: 10–15 qualified leads. Month 3: steady-state at 20–50 leads/month.

Operations

Do I need medical experience to own a med spa?

Not necessarily. Most states allow non-physicians to own med spas IF a licensed Medical Director oversees clinical operations. Requirements vary by state. Always consult a healthcare attorney during due diligence. The business side — marketing, operations, finance — doesn't require clinical training.

What are the biggest risks in med spa acquisition?

(1) Provider dependency — if the lead injector leaves, revenue walks. (2) Non-compliance — improper medical director arrangements can shut you down. (3) Aging equipment — 5+ year old lasers = $100K+ replacement. (4) No marketing system — buying a practice with no documented patient acquisition is buying a job, not a business.

How do I finance a med spa acquisition?

SBA 7(a) loans (up to $5M, 10–25 year terms), seller financing (20–40% seller note), earnouts (contingent on post-close performance), and PE/family office backing for multi-practice roll-ups. Med spas qualify as healthcare businesses under SBA guidelines.
