Frequently Asked Questions

Direct answers for med spa acquirers — based on 200+ practice transactions.

How much does it cost to buy a med spa? +
Med spas typically sell for 2.5–4.5× SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings). A $1.5M-revenue practice at 30% margins ($450K SDE) trades between $1.1M–$2.0M. Practices with AI acquisition systems command 0.5–1.0× higher multiples. SBA 7(a) financing covers 80–90% with 10–20% down. Seller notes and earnouts reduce upfront capital further.
How do I find med spas for sale? +
The best deals come through relationships, not public listings: healthcare M&A brokers, vendor reps (Allergan, Galderma), direct outreach to practice owners, aesthetic conferences. Most profitable acquisitions are off-market. Full sourcing guide →
What is AI patient acquisition? +
A 4-layer system: AI chatbot qualifies leads 24/7, automated SMS/email nurtures and books consultations, predictive ads optimize spend, and retention models flag at-risk patients. Cost: $40–$120/lead vs. $185–$320 industry average. 287% conversion improvement documented.
How long does deployment take? +
7 days from kickoff to live. Day 1: audit/strategy. Days 2–5: build landing page, configure AI chatbot, wire SMS/email, set up CRM. Days 6–7: test, launch, verify tracking. Month 1: 10–15 qualified leads. Month 3: steady-state at 20–50 leads/month.
Do I need medical experience to own a med spa? +
Not necessarily. Most states allow non-physician ownership with a licensed Medical Director overseeing clinical operations. Requirements vary by state — always consult a healthcare attorney. The business side (marketing, operations, finance) requires no clinical training. More operational FAQ →
