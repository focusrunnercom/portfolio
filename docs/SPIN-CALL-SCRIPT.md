# SPIN Call Script — Miami Med Spa Owners
## Framework: SPIN Selling + Challenger Sale
## Offer: $2,500 setup / $2,500/mo — 15 qualified leads in 30 days guarantee
## Author: FocusRunner Sales
## Date: 15 May 2026

---

## RULE OF THIS CALL

You are NOT selling a product. You are diagnosing a revenue leak. Your only goal: book a 15-minute screen share audit where you show them their actual numbers.

**25 dials → 8 answers → 2 audits booked → 1 qualified lead in pipeline**

---

## PHASE 1 — GATEKEEPER BYPASS

**Script:**
> "This is [Your Name] with FocusRunner — calling about your lead-response process. Is [Owner Name] available? Quick operations question — 45 seconds."

**If they ask "what's this regarding":**
> "I'm running a lead-response benchmark for Miami med spas. I have data on [Practice Name]'s neighborhood and want to share a comparison with [Owner Name]. 60 seconds."

**If they say "not available":**
> "No problem. I'll leave a voicemail. Quick question — is [Owner Name] the best person to talk about patient acquisition, or should I ask for the marketing director?"

---

## PHASE 2 — SITUATION (Build context, establish baseline)

**Goal:** Get them to articulate their current process. Show you understand their world.

**Opening — Owner on the line:**
> "[First Name], [Your Name] with FocusRunner. I'm calling med spa owners in [Neighborhood] this week — running a lead-response benchmark. Quick question: how do you currently handle incoming leads — calls, DMs, web forms?"

**Listen for:** Do they have a system? Is it manual? Do they respond themselves?

**Follow-up (gentle probe):**
> "And what are you spending on ads per month? Rough numbers — I'm benchmarking against similar Miami practices."

**Challenger move (if they deflect):**
> "Let me simplify. Three numbers: what you spend on ads, how many leads come in a month, and how many actually book. If you don't have those numbers on hand, that's the first thing I'd fix before spending another dollar on Meta."

**Why this works:** You're not selling. You're diagnosing. The owner respects someone who asks smart questions before pitching.

---

## PHASE 3 — PROBLEM (Find the gap, create discomfort)

**Goal:** Surface the specific failure in their lead response. Make it personal.

**Problem Question:**
> "What happens when a lead DMs you at 9 PM on a Saturday? Or fills out your web form at 11 PM on a Tuesday?"

**Listen for:** "I answer when I see it" / "My front desk handles it" / "We get to it next morning"

**Challenger Trap (if they say response time is good):**
> "Test it. Tonight at 8 PM, have someone DM your practice from a personal account. Time how long until you get a reply. If it's under 5 minutes, I'll drop this. If it's over 30 minutes — or you never get a reply — you owe me 15 minutes to show you what top-performing Miami med spas are doing differently."

**If they admit it's slow:**
> "You're not alone. Every med spa owner I talk to says the same thing. You're busy running a practice. But here's the math no one tells you: a lead that waits 30 minutes converts 10x worse than a lead answered in 60 seconds. By morning, that lead is your competitor's patient."

---

## PHASE 4 — IMPLICATION (Quantify the cost, make it hurt)

**Goal:** Put a dollar amount on the problem. Make it impossible to ignore.

**Script:**
> "Let me run some rough numbers for [Neighborhood]. If you're spending $[X] on ads — industry average for your area — and you're responding 30+ minutes later or the next morning..."
>
> "...you're converting roughly 5-8% of leads. The practices that respond in under 60 seconds convert 35-40%."
>
> "At $[X] ad spend, that means you're losing about 70% of your ad budget to slow response. Every single month. That's roughly $[X * 0.7] down the drain on leads that booked with someone faster."
>
> "Does that number feel wrong?"

**Challenger tension builder (if they resist the math):**
> "I'd be skeptical too. Here's what I tell every owner: don't take my word for it. Let me pull your practice data in a 15-minute screen share. I'll show you your actual lead response, your actual conversion rate, and the actual revenue leakage. If the numbers aren't alarming, I'm wrong, and you lose 15 minutes. If they are alarming, you save $[X * 0.7] a month."

---

## PHASE 5 — NEED-PAYOFF (Flip to solution, build urgency)

**Goal:** Have THEM articulate the value of fixing it. Don't pitch — let them sell themselves.

**Need-Payoff Question:**
> "If you could answer every lead — DM, call, web form — within 60 seconds, 24/7, even while you're sleeping or with family — what would that change for your practice?"

**Let them answer. Then:**
> "Right. So if I told you there's a system that does exactly that — responds in 7 seconds, qualifies hot leads, forwards only the ones worth your time — and it costs less than what you're currently wasting on slow response each month... would that be worth 15 minutes to see?"

**Close the audit:**
> "I have Monday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Which works for you?"

---

## PHASE 6 — CLOSE (Commitment to action)

**THE BOOK — Always present two options:**
> "Monday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM — which is better?"

**If they ask about pricing:**
> "$2,500 setup, $2,500/mo. 15 qualified leads in 30 days guaranteed. Three new patients covers the whole year. I'll show you the math on your numbers during the screen share."

**If they say "not right now":**
> "What changes in 3 months? Your ad spend stays the same. Your competitors using automated response are building a 90-day lead habit with the patients you'd be getting. The math doesn't get better by waiting."

**If they want "info sent":**
> "I have something better than info. focusrunner.io/lead-capture.html — three minutes. It texts you your personalized lead leakage audit in 24 hours. No follow-up emails. Just your numbers compared to Miami benchmarks."

**Zero-friction exit (no audit booked):**
> "Here's the link: focusrunner.io/lead-capture.html. Three minutes. Your name, your practice, your phone. We text you a free audit showing your exact lead leakage. If the numbers don't shock you, ignore it. If they do — reply and we fix it. Fair?"

## OBJECTION HANDLES (Scripted)

### "I'm not interested."
> "Fair. One question — do you know your current lead response time? If not, that's the one number I'd want to know if I were spending ad dollars. The audit shows it for free in 24 hours: focusrunner.io/lead-capture.html."

### "We already have a system."
> "Does it answer calls, DMs, AND web forms — all within 60 seconds, 24/7? Most systems cover one channel. If yours covers all three, you're ahead of 95% of Miami. If not, the audit will show which channel you're bleeding from."

### "Too busy."
> "That's exactly why you need this. You're too busy to answer leads fast, so you're losing revenue to faster competitors. The setup is one hour of your time. After that, the system works while you work. You end up less busy — because leads stop being a problem."

### "My partner needs to approve."
> "Perfect. Bring them to the screen share. 15 minutes. I walk through the numbers — no pitch, just data. If the math works, you're both sold at the same time."

### "Your AI sounds like a chatbot."
> "Don't take my word for it. DM the number I give you right now. Ask about pricing for Botox or consult availability. Time the response. If it sounds robotic, I was wrong. If it sounds like a real human, we talk."
